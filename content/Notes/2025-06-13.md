It’s a boring night, and I haven’t done anything remotely productive today. The holidays started over two weeks ago, and honestly, I’ve done almost nothing with them—aside from starting a Minecraft server and spending most of my time playing.

Whenever I try to start something meaningful, I lose motivation quickly. I end up procrastinating and jumping to something else. It’s like what BigBoxSWE called “**Shiny New Object Syndrome**”: the moment I see a new tech or a YouTuber drops a new video, I feel like I _have_ to switch and explore that instead. It's exhausting and weirdly addictive.

Now that I’ve written it out, I think I know a way forward:  
I’m going to start simple—with a to-do list of things I want to get done _by the end of each day_. Nothing fancy. Just focus and structure. I’ll also try limiting the time I spend on each task to keep my mind from drifting.

It’s not a perfect fix, but it’s a start.
